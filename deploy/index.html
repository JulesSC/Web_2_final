WELLNESS

Extending over 25,000 square feet of the desert, Amangiri’s destination spa looks out over the sublime tranquility of American Canyon Country. Its menu is shaped by the healing traditions of the Navajo, with treatments designed to restore hózhó – the holistic wellness system encompassing beauty, balance, harmony and health

Spread across 2,322 square metres (25,000 square feet), the Aman Spa reflects the healing traditions of the Navajo, with treatments drawing on the four elements of earth, wind, fire and water. Amangiri offers Hózhó healing, Yoga on the rocks, Water therapy and Aman Skincare in an effort to group, purify and nouraihs the body.

DINING

With floor-to-ceiling windows welcoming in the desert vista, the main Dining Room transforms seasonal, local ingredients sourced from Native American co-ops into modern dishes inspired by the culinary culture of the Southwest.

Presenting sustainable, seasonal menus inspired by the culinary heritage of the Navajo and the American Southwest, Amangiri’s main restaurant and dining venues are open to resort guests and non-residents with reservations ​(subject to availability).

EXPERIENCES

The tented Pavilions of Camp Sarika enable intimate encounters with the wonder of the wilderness, and no compromise on comfort. Framing the surrounding dunes the Suites and the Home embrace the area's raw aesthetic. Surrounded by 600 acres of undeveloped land, the four-bedroom Mesa Home offers consummate privacy just minutes from the resort.

Amangiri is within easy reach of some of North America’s most magnificent protected areas, including the Grand Canyon, Bryce Canyon, Zion National Park and Monument Valley Tribal Park, home of the Navajo Nation. Occupying 243 hectares Amangiri is as much a gateway to adrenaline-fuelled outdoor adventure as it is to the rich cultural heritage of the Navajo.
